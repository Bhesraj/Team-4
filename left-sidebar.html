How and Why we made Interactive Tic Tac Toe?

How we did this?

To create a smart and interactive Tic Tac Toe game, we utilized Python and C++ programming for the graphical interface. The game board was designed to be user-friendly, allowing players to place Xs and Os through touch. The core game logic was implemented to detect wins or draws by checking rows, columns, and diagonals. The user interface was refined with bright lights and sound effects, providing an engaging experience. Thorough testing and bug fixing were conducted to ensure the game's stability and reliability. The final product supports multiplayer functionality, enabling players to compete against each other locally or online. The game can be deployed for others to enjoy, hosted on a website for online accessibility or distributed as a standalone application.

Why we chose this?

Creating a clever Tic Tac Toe game is in line with Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 3, which is to guarantee and enhance everyone's health and well-being, regardless of age. The act of designing a game that tests players' cognitive capacities, judgment, and strategic thinking advances mental health and cognitive well-being even if it may not appear like it has anything to do with healthcare.

This project can be used as a teaching tool to promote mental stimulation and cognitive development, both of which are essential for overall wellness. The game's integration of artificial intelligence components, such the minimax algorithm, fosters critical and strategic thinking in addition to providing entertainment. This enhances users' cognitive well-being, which is a crucial component of a happy and healthy existence as per SDG 3.
